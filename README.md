OmniBins: An IoT-Based Smart Waste Management System Integrating Load-Sensing and Odor-Triggered Neutralization

OmniBins is a comprehensive Internet of Things (IoT) solution designed to revolutionize municipal waste management by transitioning from rigid fixed schedules to an optimized, data-driven logistics model. This system integrates real-time physical monitoring with automated environmental safety mechanisms to enhance efficiency, reduce operational costs, and safeguard public health.The Problem OmniBins Solves

Traditional waste management systems in high-traffic urban areas struggle with a lack of real-time visibility, leading to inefficient resource use and recurring environmental hazards.
Logistical Inefficiency: Collection routes are fixed, forcing trucks to visit empty bins ("ghost" stops) while hazardous, overflowing containers are neglected, resulting in wasted fuel and maintenance costs.
Public Health Risks: In tropical climates, organic waste rapidly decomposes, releasing hazardous gases like Ammonia (NH₃) and Methane (CH₄) that create "stench hotspots," posing respiratory risks to nearby residents and sanitation workers.
Worker Safety: Sanitation workers rely on manual reporting, often encountering loads that exceed physical limits, leading to long-term physical injuries from "blind lifting".


Core Features and Technology:

The OmniBins system is designed to provide proactive management and mitigation using a multi-sensor array and centralized intelligence.
1, High-Precision Load-Sensing: Utilizes high-capacity load cells to monitor the bin's physical weight in real time. This prevents bins from exceeding manageable limits for sanitation staff, enhancing worker safety and allowing the system to prioritize collection based on true physical capacity.
2, Rot Index for Chemical Urgency: Employs a gas-sensing array (MQ-135 and MQ-4 sensors) to detect and measure hazardous concentrations of decomposition gases (Ammonia and Methane). This data is translated into a "Rot Index," which allows the Local Government Unit (LGU) to prioritize collection based on chemical urgency rather than volume alone.
3, Automated Odor Mitigation: When the Rot Index breaches a specific environmental threshold, the system automatically triggers a neutralizing mist and filtration process to suppress odors and disinfect the area until collection occurs.
4, IoT Dashboard and Dynamic Routing: All data is transmitted via a reliable LoRa Star Topology network to a centralized IoT dashboard. This interface features a Geospatial Map that provides LGU personnel with real-time "Stench Mapping" and weight alerts, enabling "Operational Lean" strategies and optimized, dynamic collection routes.
5, Autonomous Operation: The hardware is powered autonomously by integrated solar panels and battery storage, ensuring continuous 24/7 operation in outdoor public areas without reliance on the public electrical grid.
